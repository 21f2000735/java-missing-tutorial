# OCJP Track

Use this path if your main goal is certification-style confidence and structured revision.

## Why This Track Exists

OCJP learners usually fail for one of two reasons:

- they study features without a stable order
- they revise too widely instead of revising the most exam-sensitive topics repeatedly

This track keeps the reading order narrower and more disciplined than the full book.

## Best Reading Order

1. `sec01_fundamentals`
2. `sec02_collections`
3. `sec03_generics`
4. `sec04_streams_and_functional_style`
5. `sec11_exception_handling`
6. `sec16_core_data_time_and_text`
7. `sec19_testing_and_quality`

## High-Yield Chapters

- fundamentals chapter 1 and 2
- collections chapter 1 and 2
- generics chapter 1
- streams chapter 1 to 4
- exception handling chapter 1 and 2
- optional and date/time chapters in `sec16`

## How To Study

1. Read the chapter guide first.
2. Run `RunAllTopics.java`.
3. Read the chapter revision sheet immediately after the code.
4. Use compare pages for quick memory repair:
   - [COMPARE_COLLECTIONS.md](COMPARE_COLLECTIONS.md)
   - [COMPARE_STREAMS.md](COMPARE_STREAMS.md)
   - [COMPARE_CONCURRENCY.md](COMPARE_CONCURRENCY.md)
5. Return to the revision sheet before moving on.

## What To Ignore Initially

- deep JVM internals
- broad architecture topics
- company-style interview tracks

Those are valuable, but they are not the fastest path to certification confidence.
